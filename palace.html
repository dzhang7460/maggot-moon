Grand Reopening of the People’s Palace Brings Employment to Zone Seven

By the Director of Civic Infrastructure | ZONE SEVEN, July 10, 1956 —

The Ministry of Infrastructure officially reopened the historic People’s Palace yesterday, instantly creating thousands of new labor assignments for relocated citizens.

The massive structure, which sat derelict for years after a terrorist arson attack, has been entirely rebuilt. It now serves a highly classified, state-essential purpose. To fulfill the rapid demands of this new initiative, authorities conducted mandatory employment sweeps across the impoverished sectors of Zone Seven, transporting able-bodied residents directly to the site.

“Work brings salvation. Those assigned to the Palace will contribute directly to the Motherland’s greatest triumph. They will live on-site to maximize efficiency and maintain operational security.” — Senior Investigative Officer, Ribbon-Cutting Ceremony
